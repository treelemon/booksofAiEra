# Ilya Sutskever

**Role:** Co-Founder & Chief Scientist, Safe Superintelligence (SSI); Former Chief Scientist, OpenAI
**Active:** 2010s–present
**Key contribution:** Co-invented AlexNet (2012) and Seq2Seq learning (2014); as OpenAI Chief Scientist, drove GPT series and the scaling hypothesis; left OpenAI in 2024 to found SSI focused on safe superintelligence

## Background

Sutskever was a co-author on AlexNet (2012), the paper that kicked off the deep learning revolution. At OpenAI, he was the driving force behind the scaling hypothesis — that larger models with more data would continue to improve. He was central to the GPT series. In 2024, he left OpenAI to found Safe Superintelligence (SSI), a company singularly focused on building safe superintelligence.

## Key Quotes

**On scaling (2023):**
> "It might be that the scaling hypothesis is all we need. We have seen remarkably consistent improvements from scaling up. It would be foolish to bet against scale."

**On his departure from OpenAI (2024):**
> "The mission of safe AGI requires a different approach. I believe that a company fully focused on safety from the ground up is the right way to achieve it."

**On the path to superintelligence (2024):**
> "Superintelligence is coming. It will be the most impactful technology humanity has ever created. Our goal is to ensure it is safe when it arrives."

**On the singularity (2025):**
> "I think we are approaching a point where AI will be able to improve itself. When that happens, progress will accelerate dramatically. That's the singularity."

**On focus (2025):**
> "SSI is not a normal company. We don't have products. We don't have revenue. We have one goal: safe superintelligence. Every decision we make is in service of that single objective."
