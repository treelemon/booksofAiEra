# Chapter 16: AI in Creativity and Media

## 16.1 Generative Media

- **Text:** LLMs write articles, stories, poetry, screenplays, marketing copy
- **Images:** DALL-E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion generate professional-quality visuals
- **Video:** Sora, Kling, Runway generate realistic video from text prompts
- **Music:** Suno, Udio generate complete songs with lyrics, melody, production
- **Voice:** ElevenLabs, Resemble produce indistinguishable synthetic voices

## 16.2 The Authenticity Crisis

- AI-generated content floods social media, news, and publishing
- Detection tools are unreliable (arms race between generation and detection)
- Provenance standards (C2PA, watermarking) are emerging but not universal
- "Slop" — low-quality AI-generated content — degrades the information ecosystem

## 16.3 Creative Work in the AI Era

**Artists and illustrators:**
- Some adapt AI as a tool (concept art, storyboarding, iteration)
- Others reject it (ethical concerns, copyright, devaluation of craft)
- Legal battles over training data (copyright cases pending in 2026)

**Writers:**
- AI as brainstorming partner and first-draft generator
- Human writers who inject personal experience and voice are thriving
- Pure AI-generated books are detectable and perform poorly

**Musicians:**
- AI for production, mixing, inspiration
- AI-generated vocals raise copyright and identity questions
- The "dead artist" problem: AI trained on deceased musicians' catalogs

## 16.4 BookTok and the AI Book Market

- TikTok drives book sales more than any other channel
- Emotional hooks (not "buy my book") drive discovery
- AI-assisted authorship is growing but reader skepticism is high
- The bestseller differentiator: personal voice and original perspective

## 16.5 Copyright and Legal Landscape

Landmark cases in 2026 will establish precedent:
- Is training on copyrighted data fair use?
- Can AI-generated works be copyrighted?
- Who owns AI-assisted creations?
- Deepfake liability and right of publicity
