# Chapter 11: Geopolitics and AI Sovereignty

## 11.1 The US–China Dynamic

The narrative has shifted dramatically:

| Dimension | US | China |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Frontier models | Lead by ~2.7% | Near parity |
| Patents | Higher impact | Higher volume |
| Publications | Fewer, more selective | More volume, faster growth |
| Industrial robots | 30K/year | 300K+/year |
| Compute access | NVIDIA GPUs | Domestic alternatives (Huawei) |
| AI adoption | 28.3% (24th globally) | High in manufacturing, surveillance |

**Key insight:** China wins on *diffusion* (deploying AI across every sector), US wins on *frontier* (pushing the state of the art).

## 11.2 Global AI Governance

A sequence of summits has shaped the governance landscape:
1. **Bletchley Declaration** (UK, 2023): first international AI safety agreement
2. **Seoul Declaration** (Korea, 2024): interoperability between national frameworks
3. **Paris AI Action Summit** (France, 2025): shifted focus to innovation and industrial adoption
4. **EU AI Act** (2024–2026): comprehensive regulatory framework

## 11.3 AI Sovereignty

Countries are racing to build independent AI capabilities:
- **EU:** Gaia-X, EuroHPC, Mistral, Aleph Alpha
- **UK:** £100M compute fund, Frontier AI Taskforce
- **India:** Bhashini (language AI), open-source model push
- **Singapore:** 61% AI adoption (highest globally)
- **UAE:** 54% adoption, major compute investments

Compute access is becoming as strategically important as nuclear capability.

## 11.4 Export Controls and the Compute War

- US restricts advanced chip exports to China (NVIDIA H100/B200)
- China develops domestic alternatives (Huawei Ascend 910B, 910C)
- Open-source models serve as a workaround (train anywhere, deploy anywhere)
- "Compute sovereignty" initiatives in Europe, India, Southeast Asia

**The paradox:** Export controls slow China's frontier progress but accelerate their domestic ecosystem and open-source contributions.
