# Chapter 10: Open-Source vs. Closed-Source

## 10.1 The Two Camps

| | Closed-Source | Open-Source/Weight |
|---|---|---|
| **Leaders** | OpenAI, Anthropic, Google | Meta, DeepSeek, AI2, IBM, Mistral |
| **Advantage** | Capability lead, safety control | Cost, transparency, customizability |
| **Business model** | API pricing, subscription | Ecosystem, enterprise services |
| **Key concern** | Centralized control | Misuse, dual-use |

In 2026, the gap has narrowed to ~2.7% — and open-source continues closing.

## 10.2 The DeepSeek Catalyst

DeepSeek's release of R1 (January 2025) was a watershed moment:
- Frontier-level reasoning at 10–20% of the training cost
- Open weights, detailed technical report
- Triggered a wave of Chinese open-source releases (GLM, Moonshot Kimi)
- Forced OpenAI to release its first open model (August 2025)

## 10.3 The Ecosystem Today

- **Llama 5** (Meta): best general-purpose open-weight model
- **Olmo 3** (AI2): fully open (data + weights + code)
- **Granite 4** (IBM): enterprise-focused, permissive license
- **Mistral Large 3** (Mistral): competitive with frontier
- **DeepSeek V4** (DeepSeek): MoE efficiency leader
- **Phi-4** (Microsoft): small but capable (for edge/device)

## 10.4 Standards and Interoperability

Three forces define open-source AI in 2026:
1. **Global diversity:** multilingual and reasoning-tuned releases from China, Europe, US
2. **Interoperability:** runtimes aligning around shared standards (GGML, OpenXLA)
3. **Governance:** security-audited releases, transparent data pipelines, model cards

## 10.5 The Debate

**For open-source:** Faster innovation, democratized access, transparency, avoids corporate control.
**Against:** Dual-use risk (weapons, bioweapons, disinformation), no "off switch," fragmentation.

This tension will not be resolved — it's structural. Both sides will coexist.
