# Appendix C: Conversation Starters for Families

Use these questions to spark family discussions about AI, technology, and the future. No right answers — just good conversations.

## Getting Started

1. When was the first time you remember using AI? What did you think about it?

2. What do you think is the most impressive thing AI can do? What's the most scary?

3. If you could ask an AI to do anything for you, what would it be?

4. What's something you think AI will never be able to do? Why?

## AI and School

5. How are you and your friends using AI for schoolwork? Is it mostly helpful or mostly a shortcut?

6. If you were a teacher, what would your policy be on using AI for assignments?

7. When does using AI for homework cross the line from "helpful tool" to "cheating"? Where's that line for you?

8. What subjects do you think AI is best at helping with? What subjects still need a human teacher?

9. Should schools teach AI literacy as a required subject? What would you want that class to cover?

## AI and the Future

10. What job do you think will be most changed by AI in the next ten years? What job will change the least?

11. If AI could do every job that doesn't require human creativity or empathy, what jobs would be left? Would that be a good world or a bad world?

12. What skill do you think will be most valuable for your generation? What skill do you want to make sure you develop?

13. Do you think AI will create more problems than it solves, or more solutions than problems? Why?

## Ethics and Values

14. If an AI makes a mistake that hurts someone, who is responsible — the AI, the programmer, or the user?

15. Should AI systems be required to tell you when you're talking to an AI instead of a human? Why or why not?

16. Is it okay for AI to make decisions about things like who gets a loan, who gets hired for a job, or how long someone goes to prison? Where should we draw the line?

17. Should there be rules about what AI is allowed to create? For example, should AI be allowed to generate images of real people doing things they never did?

## For Families

18. What's one technology rule we have as a family that you think works well? What's one you'd change?

19. How could we do a better job of learning about AI together as a family?

20. If you could wave a magic wand and decide how AI will be used in our family, what would that look like?

21. What worries you most about the future with AI? What excites you most?

22. Imagine it's twenty years from now and you're looking back. What do you hope we did right as a family when it came to technology?

## For Parents (to reflect on)

23. What messages did you receive growing up about technology? How do those messages affect how you parent around technology today?

24. When you reach for your phone or open an AI tool, what are you usually seeking? Information? Connection? Escape? How does that compare to what your children are seeking?

25. What's one technology habit you'd like to change in yourself? How could you model that change for your family?

## Quick Tips for These Conversations

- **No judgment zone.** Let everyone speak freely without fear of punishment or criticism.
- **Listen more than you talk.** You'll learn more than you teach.
- **It's okay not to have answers.** Some of the best conversations end with "I don't know, let's think about it together."
- **Make it regular.** A single conversation isn't enough. Make technology and AI a normal part of family discussion.
- **Start simple.** Don't try to cover everything at once. Pick one or two questions and let the conversation flow naturally.
