# Foreword

If you're holding this book (or scrolling through it on a screen), you've probably noticed that the world is changing fast. Really fast. New tools appear overnight. Adults argue about what it all means. And somewhere in the middle of it all is you — whether you're twelve, sixteen, or the parent of someone who is.

This book is for all three of you.

**For middle schoolers:** You're growing up in a world that didn't exist five years ago. You might use AI for homework help, to generate images for fun, or to chat with when you're curious or bored. You've probably heard adults say things like "you need to learn this for when you grow up" — and honestly, nobody quite knows what "growing up" will look like. That's okay. This book will help you understand what's happening, what's real, and what's just hype. You don't need to be a tech genius to get it. You just need to be curious.

**For high schoolers:** You're at a crossroads. College applications, career decisions, the pressure to figure out your future — it's a lot. And now there's this giant AI thing that everyone says will change everything. Maybe you're excited. Maybe you're worried. Maybe you're tired of hearing about it. Wherever you are, this book is for you. We'll talk honestly about what AI means for your education, your future career, and your life. No sugarcoating, no doom-and-gloom, just practical things you can actually use.

**For parents:** You carry a unique weight. You want your children to be prepared, but you don't want them to be anxious. You want them to use technology wisely, but you're not always sure what "wisely" looks like anymore. You might feel behind, or overwhelmed, or like the ground keeps shifting beneath your feet. You're not alone. This book isn't going to tell you that you're doing it wrong. It's going to help you understand the landscape so you can make confident decisions alongside your kids.

I write this as someone who has spent years watching technology reshape our world. I've seen the panic cycles come and go. I've watched predictions be wildly wrong. I've also watched young people use these tools to do things that genuinely surprised me — creative things, kind things, brave things.

Here's what I believe: AI is not the enemy of human potential. It's a mirror. It reflects back what we bring to it. If we bring fear, we see threats. If we bring curiosity, we see possibilities. If we bring wisdom, we see tools.

This book is an invitation to bring all three — curiosity, wisdom, and a little bit of courage — to the table. Together, we'll explore what AI actually means for your education, your family, and your future.

The future isn't something that happens to you. It's something you help build. Let's figure out how.
