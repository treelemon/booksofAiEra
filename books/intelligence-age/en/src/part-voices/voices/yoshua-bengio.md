# Yoshua Bengio

**Role:** Founder, Mila (Quebec AI Institute); Professor, Université de Montréal
**Active:** 1990s–present
**Key contribution:** Pioneered deep learning; 2018 Turing Award winner; evolved from pure research to leading voice for AI safety, ethics, and global governance

## Background

Bengio is one of the three "Godfathers of Deep Learning" (with Hinton and LeCun). He founded Mila, one of the world's largest academic AI research groups. As AI capability grew, Bengio shifted his focus significantly from technical research toward AI safety, ethics, and governance, becoming one of the loudest voices calling for regulation.

## Key Quotes

**On deep learning's potential (2015):**
> "Deep learning is a revolution. It's changing everything we thought possible in AI. But we're still very far from human-level intelligence."

**On shifting from research to safety (2023):**
> "I spent 30 years building the foundations of deep learning. Now I feel a responsibility to ensure it's used wisely. The risks are real, and we need to take them seriously."

**On regulation (2024):**
> "We can't rely on companies to self-regulate. There's too much competitive pressure. We need independent oversight and binding regulation at the international level."

**On computing power and safety (2024):**
> "The race to build more powerful AI systems without adequate safety measures is a dangerous gamble. We need to slow down and get this right."

**On democratic governance (2025):**
> "AI governance shouldn't be decided by a few companies in Silicon Valley. We need democratic processes to determine how this technology is developed and deployed."

**On hope (2025):**
> "Despite the risks, I'm optimistic. The scientific community is increasingly engaged. Policymakers are paying attention. Public awareness is growing. We still have time to get this right — but not unlimited time."
