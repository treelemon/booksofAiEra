# Chapter 14: AI in Science and Medicine

## 14.1 Drug Discovery

AI is transforming the drug development pipeline (historically: 10+ years, $2B+ per drug):
- **Target identification:** AlphaFold, ESMFold predict protein structures
- **Molecule design:** Generative models propose novel drug candidates
- **Screen optimization:** AI predicts binding affinity, toxicity, ADME properties
- **Clinical trials:** Patient recruitment, protocol optimization, real-world evidence

In 2025, the first AI-discovered drugs entered Phase II/III trials.

## 14.2 Medical AI

- **Diagnostic imaging:** FDA-approved AI for radiology, pathology, dermatology
- **Clinical decision support:** LLMs assisting with diagnosis, treatment planning
- **Medical records:** Automated summarization, coding, prior authorization
- **Surgery:** Robotic-assisted surgery with AI planning

**Key challenge:** Hallucination in clinical settings is unacceptable. Most deployed AI is narrow (one task) rather than general.

## 14.3 AI in Research

- **Literature review:** AI can process 10,000 papers overnight
- **Hypothesis generation:** Models propose novel experiments
- **Laboratory automation:** AI-controlled robots run experiments 24/7
- **Peer review:** AI-assisted review (controversial but growing)

## 14.4 The Replication Crisis

AI's entry into science raises new concerns:
- AI-generated papers flooding journals
- "Tortured phrases" from LLM-generated text
- AI-hallucinated references and data
- Difficulty distinguishing AI-generated from human research

The scientific community is still developing norms for AI-assisted research.
