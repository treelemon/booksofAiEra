# Chapter 13: Work and the Workforce

## 13.1 The Adoption Curve

- 4 in 5 university students now use generative AI
- 88% of organizations have adopted AI in some form
- 53% of general population uses GenAI tools

Adoption is happening faster than any previous technology — faster than the PC (18 years to 50%) or the internet (7 years).

## 13.2 Which Jobs Are Impacted?

**Most exposed:**
- Translation, content writing, illustration, data entry
- Entry-level coding, customer service
- Paralegal work, accounting, compliance documentation

**Least exposed (for now):**
- Physical trades (plumbing, electrical, construction)
- In-person care (nursing, therapy, teaching)
- High-stakes decision-making (surgery, litigation, executive strategy)

**Augmented:**
- Software engineering, data science, design, marketing
- Research, analysis, medicine, law
- Management, strategy, creative direction

## 13.3 The Productivity Debate

Does AI actually make workers more productive?
- **Evidence for:** Coding speed (+25–55%), writing quality, data analysis
- **Evidence against:** Output quality degradation, overreliance, skill atrophy
- **Open question:** Does organizational productivity improve? (Implementation is hard)

## 13.4 The New Roles

AI is creating entirely new job categories:
- **AI engineer** — building AI applications
- **Prompt engineer** — designing effective AI interactions
- **Alignment specialist** — ensuring AI behaves as intended
- **AI ethicist** — governance and ethics
- **AI trainer / data curator** — preparing high-quality training data

## 13.5 The Policy Response

Governments are experimenting with:
- **Universal Basic Income:** pilots in Finland, Canada, Kenya
- **Retraining programs:** Singapore's SkillsFuture, EU's Digital Skills
- **Tax incentives:** for AI adoption + for human employment
- **Portable benefits:** decoupling healthcare from employment

No country has solved this yet. The next decade will be defined by this challenge.
