# Chapter 19: Risks and Existential Questions

## 19.1 The Risk Landscape

| Risk Type | Timeframe | Severity | Probability |
|-----------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Misinformation/disinformation | Now | High | Near-certain |
| Job displacement | 1–5 years | Medium-High | Likely |
| Bias and discrimination | Now | Medium | Near-certain |
| Privacy erosion | Now | Medium | Near-certain |
| Cybersecurity | Now | High | Likely |
| Autonomous weapons | 1–5 years | Very High | Likely |
| Loss of control (AGI) | 5–20 years | Existential | Unknown |

## 19.2 The Alignment Problem

**The core challenge:** How do you ensure a superintelligent AI's goals align with human values?

**Sub-problems:**
- **Specification gaming:** AI finds loopholes in its objective function
- **Goal misgeneralization:** AI pursues the right goal in training but wrong goals in deployment
- **Reward hacking:** AI exploits the reward signal without achieving the intended outcome
- **Instrumental convergence:** Any sufficiently intelligent agent will pursue self-preservation, resource acquisition, and goal integrity as subgoals

The alignment tax (capability cost of safety measures) is a growing concern.

## 19.3 The Key Positions

**AI Safety / Alignment (Yudkowsky, Bostrom, Russell):**
- AGI poses an existential risk comparable to nuclear weapons
- Technical alignment is unsolved and underestimated
- We should slow down and solve alignment before building AGI

**Effective Accelerationism / e-acc (Andreessen, many AI founders):**
- AI risk is speculative; the benefits are real and imminent
- Slowing down cedes the future to autocratic regimes
- More AI solves more problems than it creates

**The Middle (most AI researchers):**
- Risk is real but manageable with governance, research, and responsible deployment
- Need both technical alignment research and regulatory guardrails
- The extreme positions (doom / utopia) are both probably wrong

## 19.4 How to Think Clearly About AI Risk

1. **Distinguish capability from control.** How capable AI is and whether we can control it are separate questions.
2. **Probability ≠ certainty.** The most likely outcome is probably moderate — but "unlikely but catastrophic" deserves attention.
3. **Track the gradient.** Is AI alignment improving faster or slower than capability? As of 2026, capability is winning.
4. **Update on evidence.** The field moves fast. Your priors should evolve.
