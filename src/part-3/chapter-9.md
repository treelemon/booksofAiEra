# Chapter 9: AI for Discovery

## 9.1 The AlphaEvolve Moment

In 2025, Google DeepMind revealed AlphaEvolve — an LLM combined with evolutionary algorithms to generate novel algorithms for unsolved problems. It discovered:
- More efficient data center power management
- Optimized TPU chip scheduling

This was the first demonstration of an LLM making a genuinely novel discovery.

## 9.2 The Ecosystem Grows

Within weeks, the community replicated and extended AlphaEvolve:
- **OpenEvolve** (Asankhaya Sharma): open-source reproduction
- **SinkaEvolve** (Sakana AI): Japanese variant with different search strategies
- **AlphaResearch** (US/China collaboration): claimed improvements on AlphaEvolve's math solutions

## 9.3 Domains of AI Discovery

- **Biology:** AlphaFold (protein folding), RFdiffusion (protein design), drug candidate discovery
- **Mathematics:** conjectures, proofs, novel algorithms
- **Engineering:** chip design, materials discovery, circuit optimization
- **Climate:** carbon capture materials, battery chemistry, energy grid optimization

## 9.4 How It Works

The general pattern:
1. **Generate** candidate solutions (LLM proposes)
2. **Evaluate** against a ground-truth metric (simulation, formal verification)
3. **Iterate** — evolution or reinforcement learning over candidate space
4. **Verify** — human experts check the most promising candidates

## 9.5 The Frontier

The holy grail: AI autonomously discovers fundamental science — new physics, new mathematics, new biology. We are not there yet, but the trajectory is clear. 2026 will see significant acceleration in this area.
