# Appendix C: Recommended Reading & Resources

## Essential Books

| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|------|--------|-------------|
| *Co-Intelligence* | Ethan Mollick | Best practical guide to living and working with AI |
| *The Alignment Problem* | Brian Christian | History of AI safety and alignment |
| *Superintelligence* | Nick Bostrom | The foundational text on AI risk |
| *Life 3.0* | Max Tegmark | Broad survey of AI's societal implications |
| *AI 2041* | Kai-Fu Lee & Chen Qiufan | Scenarios for AI's future |
| *The AI Dilemma* | Juliette Powell & Art Kleiner | Navigating AI ethics |
| *Designing Machine Learning Systems* | Chip Huyen | Engineering ML at scale |
| *Build a Large Language Model (From Scratch)* | Sebastian Raschka | Deep technical understanding of LLMs |
| *Grokking AI Algorithms (2nd ed.)* | Rishal Hurbans | Visual, intuitive AI literacy |
| *Empire of AI* | Karen Hao | The story of OpenAI |

## Essential Papers

- **Attention Is All You Need** (Vaswani et al., 2017) — The transformer paper
- **Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models** (Kaplan et al., 2020) — Why scale works
- **Training Language Models to Follow Instructions with RLHF** (Ouyang et al., 2022) — InstructGPT
- **Chain-of-Thought Prompting** (Wei et al., 2022) — Reasoning breakthrough
- **Constitutional AI** (Bai et al., 2022) — Anthropic's alignment approach
- **DeepSeek-R1** (2025) — Open-source reasoning model
- **The WMDP Benchmark** (2024) — Measuring dangerous capability
