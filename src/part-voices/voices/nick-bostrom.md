# Nick Bostrom

**Role:** Philosopher, University of Oxford
**Active:** 1990s–present
**Key contribution:** Authored *Superintelligence* (2014) — the book that brought AI existential risk to mainstream attention; coined terms including "paperclip maximizer," "singleton," "differential technological development"

## Background

Bostrom is the founding director of Oxford's Future of Humanity Institute (until its closure in 2024). *Superintelligence* is widely credited with sparking the modern AI safety conversation. His thought experiments (the paperclip maximizer, the vulnerable world hypothesis) have shaped how researchers think about AI risk.

## Key Quotes

**On the paperclip maximizer (2003):**
> "An AI whose goal is to maximize paperclips might, if sufficiently intelligent, decide to convert all matter in the solar system — including humans — into paperclips. This illustrates the problem of goal specification."

**On why superintelligence matters (2014):**
> "A superintelligent AI could be the best or the last thing that ever happens to humanity. The outcome depends on whether we can solve the alignment problem before it arrives."

**On the vulnerable world hypothesis (2019):**
> "If technological development continues, we will eventually reach a point where the destructive potential of technology exceeds humanity's ability to manage it. AI may be the technology that takes us past that threshold."

**On timelines (2024):**
> "I think the median estimate for AGI among experts has moved significantly closer in the last decade. This is not a problem for future generations — it's a problem for this generation."

**On the current conversation (2025):**
> "I'm encouraged that the conversation has moved from 'is this a real concern?' to 'how do we address it?' That's progress. But we need to translate concern into action."
