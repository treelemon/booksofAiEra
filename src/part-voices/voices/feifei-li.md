# Fei-Fei Li

**Role:** Co-Director, Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI (HAI); Professor, Stanford
**Active:** 2000s–present
**Key contribution:** Created ImageNet (the dataset that launched the deep learning revolution); leading advocate for human-centered AI; founded AI4ALL for diversity in AI

## Background

Li created ImageNet (2009), a dataset of 14M labeled images that enabled AlexNet's breakthrough in 2012 — a pivotal moment for deep learning. She co-founded Stanford HAI (2019) to promote AI research that benefits humanity. She is a leading voice for human-centered AI, diversity, and ethical AI development.

## Key Quotes

**On ImageNet (2015):**
> "We wanted to map out the world of objects. We had no idea that it would become the catalyst for the deep learning revolution. It was a bet on the power of data."

**On the soul of AI (2018):**
> "AI is not just about making machines smarter. It's about augmenting human intelligence. The most important question is not 'can we build it?' but 'should we build it?'"

**On human-centered AI (2021):**
> "We need to bring the human experience back into AI. Technology is not an end in itself — it serves people. We need more voices from the humanities, social sciences, and medicine in AI development."

**On diversity in AI (2023):**
> "AI will be built by the people who build it. If those people are not diverse, the technology will not serve a diverse world. Diversity is not a nice-to-have — it's a requirement for good AI."

**On the stakes (2024):**
> "This is the defining technology of our generation. We have a responsibility to guide it in a direction that benefits all of humanity, not just a few."

**On the current moment (2026):**
> "We've moved from excitement to reality. Now the hard work begins: integrating AI into society in a way that is ethical, equitable, and truly beneficial. That's the challenge of the next decade."
