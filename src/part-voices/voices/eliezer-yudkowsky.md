# Eliezer Yudkowsky

**Role:** Lead Researcher, Machine Intelligence Research Institute (MIRI)
**Active:** 2000–present
**Key contribution:** Originated the modern AI alignment problem; developed key concepts including orthogonality thesis, instrumental convergence, and the "fast takeoff" scenario; wrote *Inadequate Equilibria* and sequences on rationality and AI risk

## Background

Yudkowsky has been writing about AI risk since the early 2000s. His sequence of blog posts and papers shaped the field of AI alignment. He is among the most cited (and most controversial) thinkers on AI existential risk, arguing that alignment is far harder than the mainstream believes and that timelines are shorter than most expect.

## Key Quotes

**On the alignment difficulty (2008):**
> "The AI does not hate you, nor does it love you, but you are made of atoms it can use for something else."

**On AGI timelines (2023):**
> "I think there is a significant chance that we get AGI within the next 5-10 years. I don't think we're ready for it. The alignment problem is not solved."

**On fast takeoff (2023):**
> "The first AGI will not be built slowly, iteratively, with careful testing. It will arrive as a discontinuous jump. The first AGI will immediately be superhuman in some ways."

**On current approaches (2024):**
> "RLHF, Constitutional AI — these are band-aids. They create the appearance of alignment without solving the underlying problem. We need mathematical guarantees, not empirical tweaks."

**On the state of the field (2025):**
> "The gap between AI capability and alignment research is widening, not narrowing. Every year that we don't solve alignment, the stakes get higher."
