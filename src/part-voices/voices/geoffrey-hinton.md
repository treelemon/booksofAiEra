# Geoffrey Hinton

**Role:** Professor Emeritus, University of Toronto; "Godfather of AI"
**Active:** 1970s–present
**Key contribution:** Pioneered backpropagation, deep belief networks, and modern deep learning; won the 2018 Turing Award (with Bengio and LeCun); spent a decade at Google Brain before leaving in 2023 to speak freely about AI risk

## Background

Hinton has been called the "Godfather of Deep Learning." His 1986 backpropagation paper is foundational to modern AI. He spent 10 years at Google (2013–2023) before leaving to warn publicly about the risks of AI — a move that made global headlines. His reversal from optimist to alarmist is one of the most significant shifts in AI's public discourse.

## Key Quotes

**On backpropagation (1986):**
> "We discovered a simple way for neural networks to learn. We had no idea it would lead to this."

**On deep learning's triumph (2015):**
> "I think deep learning is going to be able to do pretty much anything. We're going to the moon."

**On leaving Google to warn the world (2023):**
> "I want to talk about AI safety without worrying about the impact on Google. I want to be able to say 'I don't think we should scale this further' without having to worry about my employer."

**On existential risk (2023):**
> "It's hard to see how you can prevent bad actors from using AI for bad things. The idea that this technology will stop being dangerous because we just need to stop developing it — that's not realistic. The genie is out of the bottle."

**On AI understanding (2024):**
> "People who say these are just stochastic parrots are wrong. They're not just predicting the next word. They have genuine understanding — it's not the same as human understanding, but it's understanding nonetheless."

**On the probability of human extinction (2025):**
> "I now think there's a 10–20% chance that AI will cause human extinction within the next 30 years. Before I left Google, I thought it was much lower."
