# Max Tegmark

**Role:** Professor, MIT; President, Future of Life Institute (FLI)
**Active:** 2000s–present
**Key contribution:** Co-founded the Future of Life Institute; authored *Life 3.0* (2017) — a comprehensive exploration of AI's societal implications; leading voice for AI governance and beneficial AI development

## Background

Tegmark is a physicist and cosmologist who turned his attention to AI in the 2010s. He co-founded FLI, which organized the first major conferences on AI safety and published the open letter calling for research on "robust and beneficial AI" (2015). *Life 3.0* remains one of the most accessible books on AI's long-term implications.

## Key Quotes

**On the open letter (2015):**
> "We got over 10,000 signatures including Hawking, Musk, and Wozniak. It showed that the AI research community takes safety seriously. That was a turning point."

**On the AI stakes (2017):**
> "AI is the most important conversation of our time. It has the potential to solve our biggest problems or create new ones beyond our imagination. The outcome depends on the choices we make today."

**On the Future of Life Institute's role (2023):**
> "Our goal is to ensure that AI is developed in a way that benefits all of humanity. We're not anti-AI — we're pro-responsible-AI."

**On the pause letter (2023, calling for a 6-month moratorium on giant AI training runs):**
> "We asked for a pause. Not a stop. A pause — to catch our breath, to develop safety standards, to get governance in place. The response showed how polarized this conversation has become."

**On governance (2024):**
> "We need to build institutions that can manage the development of superhuman AI. This is not just a technical problem — it's an institutional design problem of the highest order."

**On optimism (2025):**
> "I'm optimistic because I see what's at stake, and I see smart people working on it. But optimism without action is just wishful thinking. We need to build the governance structures *before* we need them."
