# Kai-Fu Lee

**Role:** Chairman & CEO, Sinovation Ventures; former President, Google China
**Active:** 1990s–present
**Key contribution:** Bridged US and Chinese AI ecosystems; authored *AI Superpowers* (2018) and *AI 2041* (2021); leading analyst of China's AI strategy and US-China AI competition

## Background

Lee led Microsoft Research Asia, founded Google China, and later founded Sinovation Ventures. *AI Superpowers* was among the first books to analyze the US–China AI race. *AI 2041* (with Chen Qiufan) used fiction to explore AI's potential futures. He is a unique voice with deep experience in both US and Chinese tech.

## Key Quotes

**On the US–China AI race (2018):**
> "China has three advantages: massive data, fierce entrepreneurial energy, and strong government support. The US has a lead in fundamental research and talent. The race is closer than most people think."

**On AI's impact on work (2018):**
> "AI will displace many jobs, but it will also create new ones. The challenge is not the technology — it's the transition. We need to think about education, social safety nets, and retraining."

**On the open-source wave (2025):**
> "DeepSeek showed something important: Chinese AI companies can compete on the frontier, not just on application. The open-source model is accelerating China's AI ecosystem faster than export controls can slow it."

**On AGI timelines (2025):**
> "I think AGI is further away than the optimists claim — maybe 20-30 years. But even narrow AI will transform the economy in ways we're not prepared for."

**On coexistence (2026):**
> "The US and China will both produce frontier AI. They will compete, but they need to cooperate on safety. AI risk does not respect borders. We need a framework for global AI governance."
