# Yann LeCun

**Role:** VP & Chief AI Scientist, Meta; Professor, NYU
**Active:** 1980s–present
**Key contribution:** Pioneered convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for computer vision; core developer of PyTorch; 2018 Turing Award winner; leading voice against AI existential risk narratives

## Background

LeCun invented CNNs (LeNet, 1989) — the foundation of modern computer vision. He joined Facebook (now Meta) in 2013 to lead AI research and has been a leading voice for the "AI is not an existential risk" position, arguing that intelligence does not imply a drive for dominance.

## Key Quotes

**On CNNs (1989):**
> "We showed that neural networks could recognize handwritten digits. Everyone said it was a toy. Thirty years later, every phone in the world uses this technology."

**On existential risk claims (2023):**
> "The idea that AI poses an existential threat — I think that's wildly overblown. Intelligence has nothing to do with dominance. Humans have approximately the same cognitive architecture and we don't all want to dominate each other."

**On AI understanding (2023):**
> "Large language models have no understanding of the world. They have no grounding in reality. They are brilliant parrots. That's changing, but we're not there yet."

**On open-source AI (2024):**
> "Open-source platforms like Llama are essential. They democratize AI research and prevent power from being concentrated in a few companies. The best AI will be open."

**On the path forward (2025):**
> "The next big breakthrough won't come from scaling language models. It will come from objective-driven AI — systems that learn world models from observation and can plan and reason like animals and humans do."

**On regulation (2025):**
> "We need regulation, but it should target applications, not research. Regulating research is a bad idea — it will only concentrate power and slow progress toward beneficial AI."
